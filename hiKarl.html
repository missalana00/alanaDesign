Case Study


Hi Karl

Your evening genie for personalized event recommendations.






Selected screens from Prototype.









Overview & Roles




Overview

Hi Karl is a virtual concierge that offers personalized music event recommendations directly from Facebook messenger via an easy, natural language based interface.

By connecting with users' Spotify accounts, behavioral and geographical data, Hi Karl ensures ease of discovery of relevant, nearby music events.

You can ask Karl what’s happening in town, who’s playing at a specific venue, or where to hear some good #indie tunes, and Karl will get back to you in seconds.


Roles

UX Researcher & Designer


Project

With this project, an existing design had been launched in select cities. In hopes of better serving the target audience and improving the overall user experience, my role was to direct a research validated redesign that would accomplish these goals.

Framing the Challenge




How Might We?

Following a project kickoff meeting with stakeholders, I began the discovery process by framing the project challenge in the most human way I could. What followed was a refining of the challenge into a problem statement that would drive toward the ultimate impact, allow for a variety of solutions, and take context into consideration.







Problem statement study. Used post-it notes to quickly jot down ideas, consider all statements together, and easily remove insufficent answers and insert better ones where necessary.

Understanding the Ultimate Impact

What's the Top of the Mountain Here?

To gain a firm grasp of what I was trying to accomplish and how success might later be measured, I sought to understand the company's desired ultimate impact. A wise design instructor I had phrased the consideration like this:



"How will you know you 've achieved everything you can imagine and that it's 'all down hill from here'? "

Preparing for Research




Scripting Success

To ensure the validity of the problem statement defined in the discovery phase and optimize the design work ahead, I created user interview guides for strategic customer discovery and conducted qualitative research with seven prospective users. All questions were carefully scripted ahead of time so as to elicit the most useful and unbiased responses from participants. Interviews were audiotaped with subject permission for later review.

Participants were also asked to engage with the application as it currently existed to allow me the opprtunity to observe the interaction.






Selected pages from user interview guide.









Mining For Patterns




Identifying Themes & Extracting Insights

While listening to playback of each audiotaped session, I quickly wrote down subject quotes, frustrations, motivations and other informational tidbits on post-it notes. After then sorting these notes by affinity, a number of thematic patterns surfaced that helped me then go on to build a lean persona of the user I was designing for.








Three of eight themes uncovered in the research phase. Design features of the existing application clearly spoke to some of the themes outlined; however, many themes arose that had not been addressed in the existing design. For example, the existing design recommended music events associated with the user's Spotify listening habits but provided no way for users to make a quick mental association of what the recommended event artist sounded like. Consequently, confidence in the prospect of enjoying an event was diminished and resulted in a user being less likely to follow through and attend the event.





Building a Lean Persona




Getting Off on the Goal-Directed Foot

The thematic patterns identified in my research findings were then synthesized to generate a representative persona encompassing personality, demographics, key behaviors, common goals, frequent frustrations, and psychological motivations. This deliverable served as a foundation for moving forward with goal-directed design in mind.






According to Alan Cooper, author of goal-directed design methodology, it is when your persona archetype's needs are satisfied in multiple scenarios that you may consider yourself on the right track.





Examining the Existing Design




Content Sampling

To identify what research validated user goals the existing design met and what goals had no identifiable solution, I completed a quick content sample. While a highly detailed content audit is ideal, a quick top-level inventory of what the existing design included was sufficient to spot what goals were being left unaddressed.

Sketching Out Solutions




Low-Fi First

Before entertaining the idea of wireframes, I quickly iterated over design ideas by sketching them out by hand. The main purpose of this phase is to consider the content and focus on the general structure of information before investing time in a more mid-fidelty deliverable.




Keeping it simple.





Wireframing for Structure & Function




Fast, Efficient, & Enough

Using a monochrome wireframe (over colored or grayscale) to force viewers to focus on element structure and arrangement rather than style, I utilized the design software Sketch to quickly produce a wireframe that prioritized visual clarity and function.

I included both existing widgets and new, research validated additional widgets in the wireframe. After making necessary revisions and reaching stakeholder consensus, I moved on to producing a prototype .






Winning at Wireframes: No style is the best style.





Process Reflections




Empathize More with Promotors

The case study currently focuses on the user searching for music events. More time with the project would have allowed me to empathize more with promotors utilizing Hi Karl to push last-minute tickets to fans.


Flesh Out Messenger Interaction

Users interact with Hi Karl via a natural language interface on Facebook Messenger. For future iterations, I'd like to take more time to study those message based interactions and the laguage used.
